# Our Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how **Akukuranut Development Trust (ADT)** ("ADT", "we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Cookies help us provide a secure, reliable website experience and understand how our content is used so we can improve our communications about ADT’s mission and programs.

## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, and tags.

## 2. How We Use Cookies

We may use cookies to:

- Enable core site functionality (security, page navigation)
- Remember preferences (where applicable)
- Measure site performance and understand usage trends (analytics)
- Improve our content, communications, and user experience

## 3. Types of Cookies We May Use

### 3.1 Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are required for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services.

### 3.2 Performance and analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website (e.g., which pages are most visited, how users navigate), so we can improve the site.

Where possible, we aim to use aggregated insights and avoid collecting unnecessary personal data.

### 3.3 Functionality cookies

These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as preferences) and provide enhanced features.

### 3.4 Third-party cookies

Some content or features may be provided by third parties (e.g., embedded media, analytics, social platforms). These third parties may set their own cookies, governed by their own policies.

## 4. Managing Cookies

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. You can typically:

- Delete cookies
- Block all cookies
- Block third-party cookies
- Receive a notification before a cookie is stored

If you disable cookies, parts of the website may not function properly.

## 5. Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a “Do Not Track” (DNT) signal. Because there is no consistent industry standard for DNT signals, our website may not respond to DNT in a uniform way.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Updates become effective when posted.
