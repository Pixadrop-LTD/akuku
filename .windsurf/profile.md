# **AKUKURANUT DEVELOPMENT TRUST (ADT)**

**Corporate Profile & Institutional Framework**

## **1\. Executive Summary**

Akukuranut Development Trust (ADT) is a premier local developmental Trust dedicated to creating a structured environment for community participation in socio-economic mobilization. Founded in 1989 and formally registered in 2001 under the Trustees (Perpetual Succession) Act, Cap 164 of the Laws of Kenya, ADT operates as a bridge between local human capital and external resources to mitigate the systemic challenges of poverty, disease, and illiteracy in Western Kenya.

## **2\. Institutional Identity**

### **Vision**

An empowered and organized community living free from hunger, diseases, and poverty.

### **Mission**

To provide a robust platform for community groups to share experiences and conduct critical analysis to find practical, sustainable solutions to development challenges.

### **Core Values**

* **Accountability & Transparency:** Maintaining the highest standards of resource stewardship.  
* **Professionalism:** Excellence in service delivery and technical implementation.  
* **Teamwork:** Leveraging collective intelligence for community impact.  
* **Dedication & Commitment:** Unwavering focus on the duty to the community.  
* **Respect for Human Dignity:** Upholding and protecting the rights of all individuals, particularly women and children.

## **3\. Strategic Approach**

ADT employs a multi-faceted methodology to ensure sustainable development:

* **Participatory Methods:** Engaging the community as active architects of their own development rather than passive recipients.  
* **Integrated/Holistic Approach:** Addressing the intersectionality of health, education, and economic stability.  
* **Collaborative Governance:** Partnering with the Government of Kenya, Civil Society Organizations (CSOs), and the Private Sector.  
* **Capacity Building:** Continuous professional development for employees and community caregivers.

## **4\. Operational Domains & Program Impact**

ADT currently partners with **Moi University College of Health Sciences (MUCH)** to implement the **USAID 4TheChild** initiative, supporting over **6,708 OVC from 2,765 households** across Teso South, Teso North, Teso Central, and Butula sub-counties.

### **A. Healthy Domain**

ADT focuses on ending the HIV/AIDS epidemic through aggressive community action:

* **Clinical Support:** HIV screening, referral services, and assisted disclosure.  
* **Retention in Care:** Facilitating transport for Children Actively Living with HIV (CALHIV) and PMTCT mothers for VL uptake and drug refills.  
* **Psychosocial Support:** Utilizing "Play Therapy" (supported by USAID 4TheChild) at high-volume facilities like Khunyangu Sub-County Hospital to improve treatment adherence.  
* **Nutrition:** Comprehensive nutritional assessments for children under five.

### **B. Schooled Domain**

To break the cycle of illiteracy, ADT facilitates school retention through:

* **Direct Support:** Provision of school fees, levies, uniforms, and scholastic materials.  
* **Health & Dignity:** Distribution of sanitary towels to keep adolescent girls in school.  
* **Strategic Referrals:** Linking students to major scholarship funds including Elimu, Jomo Kenyatta Foundation, and Equity’s "Wings to Fly."

### **C. Stable Domain (Economic Empowerment)**

ADT shifts households from dependency to self-reliance through:

* **Financial Inclusion:** Mobilizing 584 caregivers into 117 Saving and Loaning Groups (VSLAs).  
* **Livelihood Support:** Training in financial literacy and small business management; providing productive assets like sewing machines and salon kits.  
* **Agricultural Value Chains:** Linking 89 households to drought-resistant crops (Nyota beans, sorghum, millet, and maize) in partnership with KALRO and Farm Inputs Promotion Africa.

## **5\. Sustainable Investment Projects**

Unlike traditional NGOs, ADT invests in sustainable economic engines:

* **ADT Beekeeping Project:** A low-capital, high-yield initiative in partnership with KCDF, targeting widows, widowers, and HIV-affected households to provide sustainable income.  
* **ADT SACCO:** A financial vehicle providing credit and savings facilities to community members and case workers.  
* **World Bicycle Relief (WBR) Partnership:** Enhancing the mobility of case workers through a co-financing model, increasing the frequency and quality of household visits.

## **7\. Institutional Challenges & Growth Opportunities**

To reach its full potential, ADT is seeking support to address the following:

* **Capitalization:** Expansion of the beekeeping and honey processing value chain.  
* **Infrastructure:** Requirement for a dedicated computer lab for community learners and data entry, and spacious housing for satellite offices.  
* **Logistics:** Modernization of the apiary and acquisition of office motorbikes/vehicles for field operations.  
* **Technology:** Power backup (generators) to ensure uninterrupted service delivery.
