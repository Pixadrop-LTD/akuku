# Our Privacy Policy

**Akukuranut Development Trust (ADT)** ("ADT", "we", "us", "our") is committed to respecting your privacy and protecting personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, store, and protect personal data when you interact with ADT, including through our website, programs, partnerships, events, communications, and support channels.

ADT is a Kenyan Trust established in 1989 and registered in 2001 under the Trustees (Perpetual Succession) Act (Cap 164) of the Laws of Kenya.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to personal data processed by ADT in connection with:

- ADT websites and pages that link to this Policy
- Partner and donor engagement (including grant due diligence and reporting)
- Community programs and service delivery (including OVC case management and household resilience programming)
- Communications (email, phone, social media, in-person meetings, trainings)
- Recruitment and volunteer engagement
- Events, trainings, and workshops

## 2. Key Definitions

- **"Personal data"** means information that identifies or can identify an individual.
- **"Sensitive personal data"** may include health information, HIV status, or information about a child, where protected by law.
- **"Data subject"** means the individual whose personal data is processed.
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion, etc.).

## 4. Personal Data We Collect

The data we collect depends on how you interact with ADT.

### 4.1 Program and beneficiary data

We may process personal data to design, deliver, monitor, and improve our programs across the Healthy, Schooled, and Stable domains (including USAID 4TheChild-related implementations, referral support, scholarship linkages, and caregiver economic empowerment). This may include:

- Identity and demographic details (e.g., name, age, sex, location)
- Household and caregiver information
- Program participation and service delivery records
- Case management notes and referrals
- Education support records (school details, scholarship referrals)
- Economic empowerment activity records (VSLA/VSLAs participation, training attendance)

### 4.2 Health-related data

Where necessary for program support and referrals (and subject to additional protections), we may process health-related data, including:

- Clinical referral details and treatment adherence support records
- Maternal health support under PMTCT continuum
- Nutrition assessment results (e.g., under-five assessments)

### 4.3 Donors, partners, and suppliers

We may process:

- Names, contact details, professional titles, and organizational information
- Due diligence information required by funders/partners
- Contracting and invoicing records
- Communications and meeting records

### 4.4 Website usage data

If you use our website, we may collect:

- Device and browser information
- IP address (and approximate location)
- Pages visited and interactions
- Referring/exit pages

See our Cookie Policy for more.

### 4.5 Donations and support

If you make a donation or otherwise support ADT, we may collect:

- Donor name and contact details
- Donation amount, date/time, and reference details
- Payment channel information (e.g., M-PESA transaction reference)

We do not intentionally request or store full card numbers on our systems if a third-party payment processor is used.

### 4.6 Recruitment and volunteering

We may process:

- CVs/resumes, references, qualification and employment history
- Background or safeguarding checks where legally required or appropriate

## 5. How We Collect Personal Data

We collect personal data:

- Directly from you (forms, emails, calls, meetings)
- From community engagement and service delivery activities (including frontline case work)
- From partners (e.g., referral pathways, funders, implementing partners), where lawful
- Automatically through website technologies (cookies and similar)

## 6. Why We Use Personal Data (Purposes)

We use personal data for purposes including:

- Delivering and improving programs and services
- Case management, referrals, follow-ups, and safeguarding
- Reporting and accountability to partners and funders (including aggregated and de-identified reporting where appropriate)
- Donor engagement, communications, and stewardship
- Managing partnerships, procurement, and operations
- Recruiting staff and engaging volunteers
- Improving our website, security, and user experience
- Legal, regulatory, audit, and compliance needs

## 7. Legal Bases for Processing

Where applicable, we process personal data under one or more lawful bases recognized under the **Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019** and other applicable laws, including:

- **Consent** (e.g., for certain communications or where required for sensitive data)
- **Performance of a contract** (e.g., supplier contracts, partner MOUs)
- **Legal obligation** (e.g., statutory, audit, safeguarding requirements)
- **Legitimate interests** (e.g., operational communications, basic website analytics, security)
- **Public interest / provision of services** consistent with our charitable objectives, where applicable

For sensitive personal data (including health and children’s data), we apply **additional safeguards** and process only where lawful and necessary.

## 8. How We Share Personal Data

We may share personal data with:

- **Implementing and strategic partners** where necessary for service delivery (e.g., referral coordination with health facilities, scholarship programs, or government agencies)
- **Funders and donors** for compliance and reporting, typically in aggregated or de-identified form unless identifiable disclosure is required and lawful
- **Service providers** who support our operations (IT, hosting, communications, analytics, professional advisors)
- **Authorities** where required by law, court order, or to protect rights and safety

We do not sell personal data.

## 9. International Data Transfers

Some partners, donors, and service providers may be located outside Kenya. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place consistent with applicable law.

## 10. Data Protection for Children and Safeguarding

ADT supports children and vulnerable households. We take enhanced measures to protect children’s data and safeguard beneficiaries, including:

- Access limitation to authorized staff and approved partners
- Confidentiality obligations
- Secure handling and controlled disclosure
- Data minimization and role-based access

## 11. Data Security

We maintain administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; however, we work to continuously improve our controls.

## 12. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including program requirements, legal obligations, audit and reporting expectations, safeguarding needs, and dispute resolution.

- To access your personal data

- To object to or restrict certain processing

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details in Section 3. We may need to verify your identity before responding.
